# Phase 6 Early-Launch Legal and Business Readiness
Date: 2026-03-13
Status: Productization planning note only; not runtime truth
Scope: Minimum legal, policy, and business-readiness steps for launching Nova as early downloadable software

## Purpose
This document records the minimum legal and business-readiness packet Nova should have before a real alpha or beta launch.

This is planning guidance only.
It is not legal advice.

## Minimum Early-Launch Legal Packet
Before a real public-facing software launch, Nova should have:
- Terms of Service
- Privacy Policy
- Third-Party Licenses file
- a clear software-license position

Recommended addition:
- security contact or security-reporting path

## Why These Matter

### Terms of Service
Purpose:
- limit liability
- define acceptable use
- clarify that software is provided without broad guarantees

### Privacy Policy
Purpose:
- explain what data Nova processes
- explain what stays local
- explain what may leave the device
- explain logging and storage behavior

### Third-Party Licenses
Purpose:
- comply with open-source license obligations for shipped libraries and dependencies

### Software License Position
Purpose:
- decide whether Nova is:
  - open source
  - closed source
  - hybrid

## API and Service Compliance
Nova should also verify:
- API terms of service
- attribution requirements
- model-license permissions
- any redistribution restrictions

This is especially important if Nova is distributed to other users instead of remaining a private project.

## Company Structure Timing
An LLC is not required to share a private alpha with a few testers.

But company formation becomes much more important once Nova starts to:
- charge money
- collect payments
- sign contracts
- hire people
- raise investment

## Practical Founder Sequence
Typical practical order:
1. early alpha and product feedback
2. form company when commercialization becomes real
3. get EIN after the entity is properly formed
4. open business bank account
5. separate product operations from personal finances

## State and Federal Verification Notes
If Michigan remains the home jurisdiction, business-formation and filing requirements should be verified directly with the official Michigan LARA corporations resources and the current MiBusiness Registry Portal.

For federal tax ID setup, the IRS says entities should generally be formed with the state first, then apply for an EIN through official IRS channels.

Official verification references:
- Michigan LARA Corporations Division: https://www.michigan.gov/lara/bureau-list/cscl/corps
- Michigan MiBusiness Registry Portal information: https://www.michigan.gov/lara/news-releases/2025/06/30/michigan-launches-new-mibusiness-registry-portal-to-improve-business-filing-services
- IRS EIN overview: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/employer-identification-number
- IRS EIN online application guidance: https://www.irs.gov/Businesses/Small-Businesses-%26-Self-Employed/Apply-for-an-Employer-Identification-Number-%28EIN%29-Online

## Launch Stages

### Stage 1 - Alpha
Audience:
- technical early adopters
- local-AI users
- governance-minded testers

Goal:
- feedback and product learning

### Stage 2 - Beta
Add:
- installer
- clearer onboarding
- update path
- stronger docs
- more polished product surfaces

### Stage 3 - Public Product Launch
Only after:
- installability is stable
- update path is stable
- policies and privacy language are clear
- legal and license materials are in place

## Domain and Naming Readiness
Before public launch, Nova should also secure:
- product naming consistency
- domain ownership
- a stable website path for docs, download, privacy, and terms

## Bottom Line
Nova does not need full corporate complexity to keep moving.

But before a real public software launch, it should have:
- a minimum legal packet
- API and license compliance review
- a clear company-formation trigger
- a separation plan between personal identity and product operations
