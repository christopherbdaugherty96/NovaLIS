“Revise your previous answer considering Audit Point #2”
↓
Nova produces a new response
↓
Previous answer remains visible (history preserved)
That keeps:
authorship clear


responsibility clear


audit trail intact



5️⃣ Editing / Overriding Nova’s Response
You absolutely can allow this — safely.
Two constitutionally safe models
✅ Model A: “Edit & Replace (User-Owned)”
User edits Nova’s response


Message is marked:

 “Edited by user”


Nova does not treat it as memory


No learning occurs


This is like editing a document — not teaching Nova.

✅ Model B: “Fork & Revise”
Nova’s original response stays


New revised response is generated


Clear lineage:

 “Revised based on user-selected audit input”


This is more audit-friendly.

6️⃣ What Must NEVER Happen (Hard Rules)
To keep Nova intact, the UI must never allow:
❌ Audit layer replying automatically


❌ Audit layer replacing Nova silently


❌ Audit layer initiating changes


❌ Audit layer being “the final word”


❌ Blended voices where you can’t tell who said what


❌ Automatic preference learning from choices


If a user clicks something, that click is explicit consent — not implicit learning.

7️⃣ Why This Design Is Actually Very Strong
Most AI systems blur:
who decided


who corrected


what changed


why it changed


Your design would let Nova say, honestly:
“Here is my answer.
Here is an independent audit.
Here is what you chose to do with it.”
That’s accountability by construction.
