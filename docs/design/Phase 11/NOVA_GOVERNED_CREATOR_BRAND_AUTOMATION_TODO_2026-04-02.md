# Nova Governed Creator Brand Automation TODO
Date: 2026-04-02
Status: Future design backlog
Scope: Define a safe and monetizable path for Nova to operate as a governed content-business assistant for a creator-owned brand account

## Core Framing
If it is your account, your brand, your ideas, and Nova is the operating layer, then the right framing is:

AI-assisted content business, not fake-person botting.

The right operating split is:
- you are the creator
- Nova is the content operator

This should be treated as AI-assisted content business infrastructure under explicit human control.

## Why This Direction Fits Nova
This direction matches Nova's architecture better than deceptive social automation because it keeps:
- ownership explicit
- brand authority explicit
- approval visible
- risky public actions narrow
- automation bounded to clear operating rules

The product goal is not to impersonate a person or game platform metrics.

The product goal is to help run a compliant creator or business account as an operating layer.

## Practical Product Position
Nova can absolutely help run a creator or brand account if it behaves like a compliant publishing assistant rather than a spam bot.

The core shape should be:
- official integrations where available
- bounded automation
- visible approval points
- anti-spam posture
- no fake engagement
- no deceptive impersonation

## What You Keep Authority Over
You should retain final authority over:
- account ownership
- niche and brand direction
- final voice rules
- posting policy
- public reply strategy
- monetization decisions
- escalation on risky conversations

## Allowed Business Model
This can be a real content business system if the value comes from:
- ad revenue where eligible
- affiliate offers
- services
- digital products
- lead generation
- sponsorships

The value should not come from:
- fake engagement
- deceptive automation
- mass bot activity
- metric inflation

## Platform-Safe Boundary
The intended operating posture is:
- official APIs where available
- bounded posting rules
- bounded reply rules
- anti-spam posture
- transparent account operation where required
- no artificial engagement inflation

Nova should not be designed for:
- fake engagement loops
- mass auto-comments
- follow and unfollow loops
- fake likes, views, comments, or followers
- deceptive impersonation of a normal human account
- unofficial scraping or browser automation when a platform expects official API use
- any growth tactic that depends on spam or manipulation

The line to hold is simple:
- earn real audience response
- do not try to juice metrics with bots

## Reply Policy Tiers
Public reply handling should be split into two tiers.

### Tier 1 - auto-safe
- thank-you replies
- simple FAQ replies
- short brand-safe answers
- moderation flags
- routing of obvious support requests into review queues

### Tier 2 - approval-required
- arguments or heated exchanges
- controversial topics
- refund or service issues
- legal, medical, or financial claims
- crisis or reputation-sensitive replies
- anything that could materially damage the brand

This protects the account and keeps Nova from drifting into reckless public behavior.

## Governed Pipeline
The ideal Nova flow for this surface is:
1. create
2. approve
3. publish
4. engage
5. review

Operationally, that means:
1. idea -> script -> assets -> draft post
2. human approval of brand voice, post, and reply rules
3. publish through official platform tools where available
4. draft replies, with auto-replies allowed only in narrow safe lanes
5. summarize performance and recommend the next content batch

This should feel like:
- create: idea -> script -> assets -> draft post
- approve: human approves post and reply rules
- publish: Nova uses official posting surfaces where available
- engage: Nova drafts replies and only auto-replies inside narrow safe policies
- review: Nova summarizes performance and proposes the next batch

## Product Guardrails
This system should enforce:
- one creator or business owner of record
- one brand identity per governed lane
- one email or credential owner of record
- one primary content niche per lane
- one approved tone guide
- one posting policy
- one reply policy
- one analytics dashboard
- human override at all times

If multi-brand support ever exists, it should remain explicitly separated by workspace, policy, credentials, and approval rules.

A strong early product shape would be:
- one business or creator brand
- one content lane
- one approved tone guide
- one posting policy
- one reply policy
- one analytics dashboard

## Best Near-Term Use
If this becomes active work, the first slice should not be full social automation.

The best first slice is:
- content planning
- script and caption generation
- calendar organization
- comment triage
- reply drafting
- analytics summarization

That keeps Nova useful before widening into credentialed publishing flows.

## What To Avoid
Avoid building this as:
- a fake persona engine
- a growth-hack spam system
- a mass engagement bot
- a hidden autonomous comment loop
- a cross-platform botnet manager

Those patterns would cut directly against Nova's trust model.
