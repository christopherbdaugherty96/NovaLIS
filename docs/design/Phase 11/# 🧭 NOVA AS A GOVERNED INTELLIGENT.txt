## 🔹 PREAMBLE  

Nova’s architectural journey has always been guided by one principle:  

> **Authority first. Intelligence second.**  

Most AI systems build powerful intelligence and then attempt to control it.  
Nova does the opposite: it first constructs an **impenetrable cage of law**, then places intelligence inside it.  

This document defines the final form of that vision: **Nova as a governed intelligent agent** – an entity that can think freely, plan deeply, and choose strategies, but whose every external action is checked against user‑defined law.  

This is not a rule engine.  
This is not an autonomous agent.  
This is **a constitutional container for powerful intelligence**.  

---

## 1. CORE IDENTITY  

**Nova is an intelligent agent whose authority is fully constrained by user‑defined law.**  

- **Intelligence** may reason, plan, optimize, and choose freely – *inside the cage*.  
- **Authority** is the ability to affect the world – every such act must pass through the Governor and be explicitly permitted by law.  
- The law is **static until the user changes it** through a recorded legislative act.  

This preserves the fundamental invariant from the Blueprint:  

> **Intelligence may expand. Authority remains governed.**  

---

## 2. THE THREE‑LAYER ARCHITECTURE (FINAL FORM)  

Nova’s runtime is structured into three distinct layers, each with a constitutional role.  

### Layer 1 – Law (The Cage)  

**Definition:**  
A static, user‑owned set of rules that defines the *action space* – what Nova is permitted to do.  

Law specifies:  

- **Allowed capabilities** (from the capability registry)  
- **Forbidden capabilities**  
- **Resource limits** (time, memory, frequency)  
- **Time limits** (e.g., no actions after 10 PM)  
- **Risk thresholds** (actions requiring confirmation)  
- **Approval requirements** (one‑time tokens, policy activation)  
- **Domains of authority** (home control, calendar, etc.)  

Law does **not** prescribe *how* to act – only *whether* an action is possible.  

**Constitutional properties:**  
- Law is stored in **Ring 3 (user‑owned)** and versioned.  
- Law changes only through explicit user ratification (`POLICY_REPLACED`).  
- Law is immutable at runtime – no adaptation, no drift.  

---

### Layer 2 – Governor (The Enforcement Engine)  

**Definition:**  
The single choke point that validates every proposed action against the law.  

**Flow:**  

```
Intelligence proposes an action  
      ↓  
Governor validates:  
   - Is the action type allowed?  
   - Are all constraints satisfied?  
   - Is a valid token present (if required)?  
      ↓  
Allowed → Execute (via ExecuteBoundary)  
Denied → Refuse (log `AUTONOMOUS_ACTION_DENIED`)  
```  

**Constitutional properties:**  
- **Fail closed** – if validation fails, action is refused.  
- **No bypass** – every external effect must pass through the Governor.  
- **Full audit** – every attempt is logged in the immutable Ledger.  
- **No implicit escalation** – the Governor never retries or falls back without user confirmation.  

The Governor performs **no reasoning** – it only enforces.  

---

### Layer 3 – Intelligence (The Mind)  

**Definition:**  
The cognitive layer that operates *inside* the cage. Intelligence can:  

- **Plan multi‑step workflows**  
- **Choose strategies** to achieve a goal  
- **Optimize outcomes** (e.g., minimize energy use, maximize clarity)  
- **Decide sequencing** of allowed actions  
- **Recover from errors** by trying alternative allowed actions  
- **Reason deeply** using internal or external models  
- **Use tools** (search, summarization, etc.) as long as they are within the law  

**Crucially:** Intelligence is free to choose *how* to accomplish a task – but it cannot choose *what authority it has*.  

**Constitutional properties:**  
- Intelligence may only propose actions that are **individually legal**.  
- Intelligence may **not** expand its own authority (no self‑modification of law).  
- Intelligence may **not** create persistent goals without user approval.  
- Intelligence may **not** run in the background without explicit authorization (Tier 4 only).  

---

## 3. WHAT THIS ENABLES  

This architecture allows Nova to be **powerful without being autonomous**.  

**Example:**  

User says: “Prepare a weekly summary and send it to me.”  

Intelligence decides:  
- Which files to read (within allowed directories)  
- What to extract  
- How to structure the summary  
- Which format to use  
- When to send (e.g., Friday at 5 PM)  

Governor checks each step:  
- Read file → allowed? Yes.  
- Send email → recipient approved? Yes (user’s own address).  
- Size limits → within bounds.  

All steps execute; the user receives a summary.  

To the user, Nova appears highly capable and adaptive.  
But internally, every action was pre‑authorized by law.  

---

## 4. THE FOUR HARD INVARIANTS  

To keep the cage intact, the following are **Tier 1 – Immutable Core**:  

### 4.1 No Self‑Expanding Authority  

> **Intelligence may never grant itself new permissions.**  

Any attempt to modify the law, issue new tokens, or alter the capability registry is automatically blocked.  

### 4.2 No Hidden Action Paths  

> **Every external effect must pass through the Governor.**  

There is no “back door” – all I/O, network calls, file writes, and device controls are mediated.  

### 4.3 No Persistent Self‑Generated Goals  

> **Intelligence may not invent long‑term objectives without user approval.**  

Goals must originate from:  
- A direct user command  
- An approved policy  
- A ratified proposal  

Background cognition (Tier 4) may generate proposals, but they are non‑executable until approved.  

### 4.4 No Background Execution Without Authorization  

> **Nova may not act silently in the background.**  

All autonomous execution requires an active token or policy, and every action is logged.

## 6. THE LAW ANALOGY  

Think of Nova as a **citizen in a constitutional state**:  

- **Law** = the constitution and statutes (user‑defined).  
- **Governor** = the courts and police (enforcement).  
- **Intelligence** = the citizen, free to act within the law.  

The citizen may pursue any goal, use any strategy, make any plan – as long as every action is legal.  

This is the correct mental model for “strong intelligence in a cage.”  

---

## 7. EVOLUTION VS. OPTIMIZATION (REFINED)  

With planning intelligence, the distinction between evolution and optimization becomes even more critical.  

- **Evolution** – changing the law itself. Allowed only through user ratification.  
- **Optimization** – changing behavior without changing the law. Allowed **only within the intelligence layer**, as long as it does not alter the law or create new authority.  

Thus:  

> **Intelligence may optimize its plans and strategies. It may never optimize the law.**  

The law remains static until the user changes it.

## 9. THE NEXT GOVERNANCE FRONTIER: PLAN‑LEVEL RISK  

Once intelligence can compose multi‑step plans, a new risk emerges:  

> **Each step is legal, but the overall plan has a risky outcome.**  

Example:  
- Step 1: Read calendar (allowed).  
- Step 2: Read email (allowed).  
- Step 3: Combine and send summary (allowed).  
- **Result:** The summary contains sensitive information the user did not intend to share.  

The law allowed each action, but the composition produced an unintended effect.  

This is **plan‑level risk**, and it requires new governance mechanisms:  

- **Plan validation** – before executing a multi‑step plan, the Governor could simulate it and ask for confirmation if it exceeds a risk threshold.  
- **Semantic constraints** – law could include restrictions on information flow (e.g., “do not combine calendar and email”).  
- **User‑defined plan templates** – pre‑approved workflows that limit composition.  

This is not yet constitutionally mandated, but it is the logical next area of design.  

---

## 10. THE ULTIMATE RED LINE  

> **Intelligence may choose actions. Intelligence may not choose authority.**  

If Nova ever acts to expand its own permissions, create new goals without user intent, or hide actions from the Ledger, it has crossed the line into autonomy.  

All other capabilities – planning, optimization, strategy selection – are safe *inside the cage*.
