# Portfolio Operating Model (2026-04-22)

## Vision
Build a managed ecosystem of businesses where each brand operates independently to customers while sharing internal systems, operator oversight, and Nova as the internal intelligence layer.

## Core Structure
- Owner Layer: Christopher as owner / builder / portfolio operator.
- Revenue Engines: Pour Social, Website LLC, future ventures.
- Intelligence Layer: Nova as internal management platform.

## Revenue Engines
### Pour Social
Event bar staffing & beverage experience company.

### Website LLC
Website creation, digital presence, lead generation, and client systems.

### Future Ventures
Additional businesses added over time under the same operating framework.

## Nova Role
Nova functions internally to organize, coordinate, and improve operations.

### Nova within Pour Social
- lead intake
- quote drafts
- staffing schedules
- event checklists
- reminders
- inventory planning
- SOP storage
- dashboards

### Nova within Website LLC
- client pipeline
- proposals
- task tracking
- content drafts
- SEO checklists
- onboarding
- support queue

## Strategic Direction
1. Multi-city staffing brand potential.
2. Semi-passive operator model.
3. Shared back-end systems across brands.
4. Future ventures added over time.

## Separation Principle
Separate public brands. Unified back-end systems.
Customers see dedicated brands; operations may share tooling, dashboards, and Nova workflows.

## Execution Phases
### Phase 1
Manual systems + Nova assistance.
### Phase 2
Selective automation + centralized dashboards.
### Phase 3
Unified operating layer across businesses.
### Phase 4
Scaled portfolio with Nova coordinating internal operations.

## Core Insight
The long-term goal is not random side projects. The goal is a managed portfolio of businesses supported by one internal intelligence system.
