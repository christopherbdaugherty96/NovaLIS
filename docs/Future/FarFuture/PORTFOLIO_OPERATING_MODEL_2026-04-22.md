# Portfolio Operating Model (2026-04-22)

## Scope Boundary
This document is a long-term portfolio reference only.

Website LLC and demo-preview work should remain separate from Nova core until those lanes are stable, complete, and ready for deliberate integration.

Do not treat this document as authorization to merge business operations into Nova runtime work.

## Vision
Build a managed ecosystem of businesses where each brand operates independently in public while sharing internal systems, operator oversight, and Nova as a possible future internal intelligence layer.

The objective is long-term freedom through owned systems: multiple revenue engines, shared infrastructure, and one command layer that improves execution across ventures when appropriate.

## Core Structure

### Owner Layer
Christopher operates as the owner, builder, strategist, and portfolio operator.

Primary focus:
- direction
- sales
- standards
- growth
- partnerships
- capital allocation
- final decisions

### Revenue Engines (Priority Order)
1. Website LLC (Primary First Focus)
2. Pour Social (Secondary Expansion Engine)
3. Shopify / Ecommerce (Future Expansion Lane)
4. Nova Commercial Expansion (Future Lane)
5. Future Ventures

## Primary Engine — Website LLC
Website LLC is the first active revenue engine.

It focuses on website creation, digital presence, lead generation, and simple client systems for local businesses.

Why first:
- easiest to start
- fastest path to revenue
- strong margins
- low overhead
- clear deliverables
- can operate while maintaining a job
- builds network and referrals
- can fund later ventures

Core model:
- websites for local businesses
- lead-generation assets
- conversion-focused landing pages
- maintenance retainers
- future automation / CRM services only after the base service is proven

Revenue paths:
- one-time builds
- monthly retainers
- redesign packages
- SEO / optimization upsells
- consulting

## Secondary Engine — Pour Social
Event bar staffing and beverage experience company.

Core model:
- operator-led, not dependent on one bartender
- staffed events through hired bartenders or contractors
- owner may step in when strategic or necessary
- premium private events, weddings, and corporate functions
- scalable to multiple cities over time

## Future Lanes
Shopify, Nova commercial expansion, and other ventures remain future lanes.
They should not pull attention away from Website LLC until the first engine is stable.

## Nova Role
Nova may eventually support portfolio operations internally through organization, drafting, tracking, research, and workflow support.

For now, keep Website LLC and preview/demo work separate from Nova core until both lanes are stable and intentionally integrated.

## Separation Principle
Separate public brands. Separate current work lanes. Deliberate integration only after stabilization.

Customers should experience dedicated brands with clear offers.
Nova core should remain focused on product/runtime quality.
Website LLC should remain focused on service packaging, demos, and sales readiness.

## Operating Rules
- keep public offers simple
- avoid mixing unfinished business work into Nova core
- stabilize Website LLC before expanding lanes
- stabilize demo previews before integrating them into broader systems
- build systems before adding complexity
- launch small, improve fast
- focus on highest leverage opportunities first

## Execution Phases
### Phase 1
Website LLC primary push.
Launch offers, validate demand, close early customers, and build recurring revenue.

### Phase 2
Demo-preview stabilization.
Make demo previews polished, reliable, and ready to show.

### Phase 3
Deliberate integration planning.
Only after Website LLC and demo previews are stable, decide what belongs inside Nova and what should remain separate.

### Phase 4
Broader portfolio expansion.
Use proven systems and revenue to expand responsibly.

## Core Insight
The long-term goal is not random side projects.
The goal is a managed portfolio of businesses supported by clear systems, stable demos, and deliberate integration choices.
