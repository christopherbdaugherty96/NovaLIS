# Portfolio Operating Model (2026-04-22)

## Vision
Build a managed ecosystem of businesses where each brand operates independently in public while sharing internal systems, operator oversight, and Nova as the internal intelligence layer.

The objective is long-term freedom through owned systems: multiple revenue engines, shared infrastructure, and one command layer that improves execution across all ventures.

## Core Structure
### Owner Layer
Christopher operates as the owner, builder, strategist, and portfolio operator.

Primary focus:
- direction
- sales
- standards
- growth
- partnerships
- capital allocation
- final decisions

### Revenue Engines
- Pour Social
- Website LLC
- Future Ventures

### Intelligence Layer
Nova operates as the internal management platform across the portfolio.

## Revenue Engines
## Pour Social
Event bar staffing & beverage experience company.

Core model:
- operator-led, not dependent on one bartender
- staffed events through hired bartenders or contractors
- owner may step in when strategic or necessary
- premium private events, weddings, and corporate functions
- scalable to multiple cities over time

Revenue paths:
- staffing packages
- premium event packages
- mobile bar add-ons
- planning fees
- recurring venue / planner partnerships

## Website LLC
Website creation, digital presence, lead generation, and client systems business.

Core model:
- websites for local businesses
- lead-generation assets
- conversion-focused landing pages
- maintenance retainers
- future automation / CRM services

Revenue paths:
- one-time builds
- monthly retainers
- redesign packages
- SEO / optimization upsells
- consulting

## Future Ventures
Additional businesses launched over time under the same operating framework.

Rule: new ventures should either generate revenue, create leverage, or strengthen the ecosystem.

## Nova Role
Nova functions internally to organize, coordinate, and improve operations. Nova is not required to be the public-facing brand. Nova can quietly run internal workflows while customer brands remain simple and clear.

## Strategic Direction
1. Multi-city staffing brand potential.
2. Semi-passive operator model through systems and delegated labor.
3. Shared back-end systems across brands.
4. Future ventures added over time.
5. Strong owner leverage: less dependence on direct labor.

## Separation Principle
Separate public brands. Unified back-end systems.

Customers should experience dedicated brands with clear offers.
Operations may share tooling, dashboards, processes, automations, and Nova workflows.

## Operating Rules
- keep public offers simple
- centralize admin work internally
- automate repetitive tasks carefully
- preserve quality standards across brands
- track margins and cash flow
- build systems before adding complexity
- launch small, improve fast

## Execution Phases
### Phase 1
Manual systems + Nova assistance.
Launch offers, validate demand, close early customers.

### Phase 2
Selective automation + centralized dashboards.
Reduce repetitive admin work.

### Phase 3
Unified operating layer across businesses.
Shared metrics, shared workflows, stronger delegation.

### Phase 4
Scaled portfolio with Nova coordinating internal operations.
Owner focuses on growth, acquisitions, partnerships, and expansion.

## Core Insight
The long-term goal is not random side projects.
The goal is a managed portfolio of businesses supported by one internal intelligence system that increases leverage over time.
