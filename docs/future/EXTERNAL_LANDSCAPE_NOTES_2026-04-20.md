## Relevant External Signals

### AI agent governance and trust

NIST announced the **AI Agent Standards Initiative** on **February 17, 2026**, focused on secure adoption, interoperability, and trusted agent behavior.

Why it matters for Nova:

- Nova's trust/governance posture is aligned with a real external direction of travel
- explicit boundaries and auditability are becoming easier to explain as strengths rather than niche constraints

Reference:

- https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/02/announcing-ai-agent-standards-initiative-interoperable-and-secure

### MCP ecosystem maturity

MCP continues to mature through formal governance, accepted standards work, official extensions, and broad platform support.

Why it matters for Nova:

- governed MCP integration remains a plausible future multiplier
- MCP is important enough to track, but still should remain subordinate to Nova's current proof-of-trust priorities

References:

- https://blog.modelcontextprotocol.io/posts/2025-12-09-mcp-joins-agentic-ai-foundation/
- https://modelcontextprotocol.io/community/seps
- https://modelcontextprotocol.io/community/sdk-tiers

### Local / agent tooling landscape

The broader ecosystem contains:

- highly permissive automation-first agents
- memory-first systems
- MCP-native hosts and tooling

Why it matters for Nova:

- Nova should not try to win by matching every capability pattern
- Nova's clearer lane is governed local trust, inspectability, and bounded authority

Examples reviewed during this note (illustrative, not ranked or exhaustively benchmarked):

- OpenClaw
- memU
- nanobot

This note intentionally avoids hard-coding unstable comparative metrics such as star counts unless they are freshly re-verified at the time of use.

## Practical Interpretation

The external landscape supports three ideas:

1. Trust and governance are increasingly legible advantages
2. MCP matters, but Nova does not need to rush into it before first-run trust surfaces are solid
3. Memory systems are common; Nova should emphasize continuity that users can actually feel rather than abstract memory ambition
