## Purpose

Define a realistic pricing ladder for Auralis web services, maintenance plans, and future Nova-backed add-ons.

Pricing should remain flexible until real demand, scope, delivery time, and support burden are validated. This document is a planning framework, not a public price sheet.

---

## Core Principles

- Sell clear outcomes, not vague features.
- Keep the first customer offer simple.
- Do not underprice custom work.
- Separate one-time build fees from recurring support.
- Prefer recurring maintenance where value is real.
- Do not charge for unsupported Nova features.
- Price support burden into higher tiers.
- Keep Nova-backed offerings draft-first and approval-based until runtime maturity supports more.

---

## Pricing Assumptions

These are working assumptions only.

- Early Auralis customers will likely be small local businesses.
- Many will need education on why a website matters.
- Starter pricing must be accessible but not so low that it creates unsustainable custom work.
- Recurring maintenance is important for stable business income.
- Nova-backed services should start as managed/draft/reporting add-ons, not autonomous automation.

## Package 1: Starter Website

### Ideal Customer

Small business needing a basic professional online presence.

### Includes

- One-page or simple multi-section website
- Business overview
- Services section
- About section
- Contact section
- Contact form or inquiry link
- Mobile optimization
- Basic SEO setup
- Basic launch checklist

### Excludes

- Custom backend systems
- Advanced booking systems
- Full copywriting package unless added
- Ongoing edits after launch unless maintenance is purchased
- Nova-backed lead intelligence

### Working Price Range

TBD after local validation.

Suggested planning range: low-cost local starter package, but with a defined minimum floor.

### Notes

This package should be easy to explain and easy to close. It should not become unlimited custom work.

## Package 4: Website + Nova Lead Console

### Status

Future package. Do not sell as live until Nova Lead Console exists and passes decision gates.

### Ideal Customer

Business receiving enough inquiries that summaries, draft replies, and follow-up suggestions save time.

### Includes

- Structured lead intake
- Business profile basics
- Lead summaries
- Missing information detection
- Draft replies
- Follow-up suggestions
- Weekly lead report
- Visible record of what Nova drafted and did not send

### Boundaries

- No autonomous email sending by default
- No autonomous booking by default
- No autonomous website publishing
- Owner approval remains required

### Pricing Model

TBD.
